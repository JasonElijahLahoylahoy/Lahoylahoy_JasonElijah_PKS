## Overview
This lecture introduces the fundamental concepts of cybersecurity, including assets, vulnerabilities, threats, risks, the CIA Triad, and the MITRE ATT&CK Framework.

## Key Concepts

### Assets
Assets are anything valuable that must be protected.

#### Tangible Assets
- Servers
- Laptops
- Networking equipment
- Physical infrastructure

#### Intangible Assets
- Customer data
- Intellectual property
- Company reputation

### Vulnerabilities
Weaknesses in systems or security procedures.

#### Technical Vulnerabilities
- Unpatched software
- Misconfigured firewalls
- Zero-day exploits

#### Human Vulnerabilities
- Weak passwords
- Lack of cybersecurity awareness

### Threats
Threats are events or actors capable of causing damage.

#### Intentional Threats
- Hackers
- Ransomware groups
- Malicious insiders

#### Accidental/Natural Threats
- Human error
- Power outages
- Floods

### Risk
Risk is the probability of a threat exploiting a vulnerability.

```text
Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact
